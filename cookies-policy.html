Cookies Policy

This Cookies Policy explains how Betzzy (“we”, “our”, “us”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit betzzy.com. It sets out the types of cookies we use, their purpose, and how you can manage your preferences in line with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”) and the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC, as amended).

1. Purpose of This Policy

We are committed to transparency about how cookies operate on our site. Cookies help us deliver a secure, functional, and personalised experience while also enabling us to understand how visitors use our services.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They allow the site to recognise your device and remember certain information about your visit.

Cookies can be classified as:

  • Session cookies – Active only while your browser is open; deleted when you close it.
  • Persistent cookies – Remain on your device until they expire or are deleted manually.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We categorise the cookies on betzzy.com as follows:

a) Strictly Necessary Cookies

Essential for the operation of the site, such as enabling page navigation, secure access, and load balancing. Without these, the site cannot function correctly.

b) Performance & Analytics Cookies

Help us understand how visitors use our site (e.g., pages visited, time spent, clicks made). This information is aggregated and anonymised. We use tools such as Google Analytics to collect these insights.

c) Functionality Cookies

Enable the site to remember your choices (such as language preferences) and provide enhanced, more personalised features.

d) Advertising & Affiliate Cookies

Used to track your interaction with affiliate links to licensed Irish betting operators. These cookies allow us to confirm referrals and measure the effectiveness of promotions. They do not store personal betting activity.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third parties whose services we integrate into our site. These may include:

  • Google Analytics (traffic and performance tracking)
  • Affiliate tracking platforms (referral performance measurement)

We do not control these cookies directly and recommend reviewing their respective privacy and cookie policies.

5. Legal Basis for Using Cookies

Under GDPR, our use of non-essential cookies is based on your consent, which we request through a cookie banner when you first visit our site. Strictly necessary cookies are processed based on our legitimate interest in ensuring the website functions properly.

6. Managing & Disabling Cookies

You have the right to accept, reject, or customise your cookie preferences at any time. You can manage cookies by:

  • Using our cookie consent management tool (accessible via the banner or footer)
  • Adjusting your browser settings to block or delete cookies

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect site functionality.

Browser-specific cookie management guidance:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

7. Retention of Cookie Data

Cookie data is stored for no longer than necessary to fulfil its intended purpose. Persistent cookies have defined expiry dates, and session cookies are deleted when your browsing session ends.

8. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookies Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law, or our practices. Any updates will be posted here with an updated “Effective Date.”
